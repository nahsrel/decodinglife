what you just experienced
Humans see images, color, and composition — but the scanner sees binary contrast patterns. What looks different to us can be identical to the algorithm.

QR readers process images by converting them to black-and-white pixel maps. They ignore texture, color, and decoration as long as the data modules remain intact and contrast is high enough.

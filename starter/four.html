qr codes have a unique set of patterns, which means it cannot mix-and-match random halves.
what you just experienced
Each QR code holds a unique pattern of black and white squares — its own fingerprint. The design is so precise that there’s only one possible match that works.

Each QR pattern corresponds to a unique binary sequence derived from encoded text. When halves from two codes mix, the pattern logic (alignment, timing, data modules) breaks, making it unreadable.
Like other unique identifiers
Every pattern represents something different, and even tiny changes can mean a whole new message. Like the thumbprints on our fingers, no two are exactly the same.

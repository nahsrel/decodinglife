qr codes require some empty space around the code for it to be scanned.
what you just experienced
Every QR code needs a small white border — called a quiet zone — to mark where it begins and ends. Without that space, the scanner gets confused and can’t tell where the pattern stops.

The quiet zone should be at least 4 modules wide on all sides. Without it, the scanner might misinterpret surrounding patterns as part of the code. It’s like punctuation marks separating a sentence from its surroundings.
deeper thoughts
Empty space isn’t wasted space. Whether in design or in life, leaving room around something helps us notice and understand it more clearly.
